Tanzania is a country in East Africa with a rich history and culture. It is home to some of the most iconic national parks in the world, including Serengeti National Park, the Ngorongoro Crater, and Mount Kilimanjaro. Tanzania is also home to a diverse range of peoples, speaking over 120 languages. The most common language is Swahili, but English is also widely spoken.

People, languages, and culture

Tanzania is home to a diverse range of peoples, speaking over 120 languages. The most common language is Swahili, but English is also widely spoken. The official language of Tanzania is Swahili, but English is also widely used in government and business.

Tanzanian culture is a blend of African and Arab influences. The country is home to a wide variety of traditional dances, music, and art. Some of the most popular traditional dances include the ngoma, the taarab, and the mganda.
